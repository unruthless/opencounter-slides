The Problem

Entrepreneurship and small business development
are vital
to creating the jobs and economic activity
that define dynamic cities.

The Problem

Opening a new business in any city
requires coordination
between many different departments.

(Everything from submitting building plans and applications to securing health permits!)

The Problem

This presents a
cost-prohibitive
and confusing
hurdle to fledgling ventures.

I want to focus on growing my business, not on compliance.

Research

  • 5-week residency
  • Conducted focus groups with business owners - home occupancies, retail, food service, industrial
  • Studied the Municipal Code & zoning ordinances
  • Interviewed the Planning, Building, and Finance Departments
  • Collected forms…
  • …LOTS of forms.

Findings & Key Takeaways

  • 4700 businesses in Santa Cruz
  • Almost 50% are home occupancies, a.k.a. work-from-home
  • Business permitting is a routine interaction for government, but a complex and rare interaction for the applicant.
  • Success = humanizing the process.

What Do Entrepreneurs Want?

  • What I need to do?
  • How much will this cost me?
  • Who can help me navigate the process?
